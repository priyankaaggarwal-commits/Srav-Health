Practitioner Standards

Who we work with.
Qualified Ayurvedic Practitioners

We are selective by design. We only partner with practitioners who meet our qualification standards because your outcomes is our success.

Education

Formal Ayurvedic Training

  • Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS) from an accredited institution
  • OR equivalent 3+ year accredited Ayurvedic program in the US (NAMA-recognized schools preferred)
  • Continuing education in women's health, gynecology, or hormonal balance
  • Active membership in NAMA (National Ayurvedic Medical Association) preferred
Clinical Experience

Active Practice With Real Outcomes

  • Minimum 2 years of clinical practice post-graduation
  • Active or recent client base even if small with documented outcomes
  • Women's health as a primary or significant focus area
  • Experience with conditions such as hormonal imbalance, perimenopause, stress, gut health, or fertility support
Proof of Outcomes

Demonstrable Client Results with Ayurvedic Interventions Bodywork/Consultation/Programs/Yoga

  • Minimum 3 documented client success stories or case notes
  • At least 2 verifiable client testimonials or reviews
  • Willingness to share anonymized outcomes to support content creation
  • No active disciplinary actions or unresolved complaints
Delivery & Readiness

Virtual Ayurveda Practice or In-Person (Consultations, Bodywork, Yoga or Programs) : All Welcome

  • Currently serving clients virtually, in-person, or both anywhere in the US
  • Capacity to take on 3–8 new clients per month, or interest in launching a group program
  • Willing to invest 2–3 hours/week in the positioning and content process
  • Open to refining how you communicate your methodology for Western audiences
Our Approach for Ayurveda

How we position
Ayurvedic practitioners.

The Ayurvedic Practitioner Marketing Challenge

Ayurvedic practitioners face a unique communication challenge in the Western market. Your methodology is 5,000 years deep, but your potential clients are health-conscious, science-literate women who are skeptical of anything too esoteric, yet hungry for solutions that conventional medicine hasn't provided.

The practitioners who win this market bring together the power of evidence informed scientifically articulated integrative practices. You build trust before the first consultation or program purchase. That's the shift needed for Ayurvedic or Integrative practices positioning to be understood and adopted in mainstream.

We specialize in this translation. We help you claim a clear, confident positioning that respects your training and converts your ideal client whether she finds you on Instagram, Google, or through a referral from her GP.

Symptom-Led Messaging

We build your content around the specific conditions your clients are experiencing fatigue, hormonal imbalance, perimenopause, gut health bridging Ayurvedic concepts to felt symptoms without over-simplifying your methodology.

Evidence Bridge

We integrate current research on Ayurvedic interventions alongside your clinical experience. Your ideal client trusts both ancient wisdom and modern evidence. We help you speak both languages fluently.

Multi-Channel Authority

We identify where health-conscious women in your geography or your virtual audience spend time and build your presence there: SEO, social, community, referrals, and events. We support you with end to end AI enabled tech that includes website, client and practitioner facing portal that improves retention and AI agents.

Case Study

From 8 clients to a waitlist in 90 days

Dr. Meera P. had been practicing Ayurveda in San Francisco for four years. Strong clinical outcomes. Zero online presence. Every client came from a single yoga studio referral that had started to dry up.

She had the outcomes. She had the testimonials. She just had no system to convert them into visibility and no language that resonated with the health-literate SF women she wanted to serve.

Modality Ayurveda Women's hormonal & gut health
Location San Francisco, CA (in-person + virtual)
Timeline 90-day engagement
Starting point 8 active clients, 180 Instagram followers

The Challenge

Meera's messaging was practitioner-centric heavy on Sanskrit terminology and methodology. Her potential clients had no frame of reference. Booking page conversion rate: under 3%. Her positioning had no clear through-line from "what I do" to "what it solves for you."

What We Did

We rebuilt her positioning around lived symptoms: fatigue, irregular cycles, bloating, hormonal anxiety. We translated her Ayurvedic approach into clear, outcome-led language, redesigned her website, and built a 12-week content calendar with evidence-bridging posts. We established her presence in SF women's health communities and set up a referral loop with two local OB/GYNs.

Marketing Strategy

Which Channels to Use
Depending on Your Stage

Srav Health · February 2026 · 10 min read

One of the most common mistakes integrative practitioners make is comparing their marketing to someone who's at a completely different stage. You're 2 years into practice, still building your reputation and you're trying to replicate the content strategy of someone with a 10-year following and a team behind them. It doesn't work. And it burns you out.

The channels that matter and the way you use them should change as your practice grows. Here's how to think about it across four stages.

The question isn't "which channels are best?" It's "which channels are best for where I am right now?" The answer changes at every stage and using the wrong channels at the wrong time is expensive in both time and money.

Stage 1 Building Proof

0–15 active clients. You're establishing your methodology, gathering testimonials, and learning what resonates. Marketing budget is limited.
Priority 1

Word of Mouth + Referrals

At this stage, the fastest path to new clients is personal introductions. Systematize your referral asks every satisfied client should know exactly how to refer someone to you.

Priority 2

Google Business Profile

Free, high-intent, and often overlooked. A complete, keyword-rich Google Business Profile with even 5–10 reviews will outperform most paid advertising at this stage.

Priority 3

One Social Platform

Choose one Instagram or LinkedIn depending on your audience and post consistently 3x per week. Depth over breadth. Quality over quantity.

Stage 2 Building Visibility

15–40 active clients. You have proof points, some testimonials, and a clear niche. You're ready to build systematic lead generation.
Priority 1

Website + SEO

Now's the time to invest in a proper website with optimized pages for your specific conditions. A well-written blog post can drive qualified leads for years the best long-term ROI channel.

Priority 2

Email List

Start building your email list with a lead magnet a guide, a quiz, a checklist that solves a specific problem for your ideal client. Email converts better than social at this stage.

Priority 3

Community Presence

Identify 2–3 online communities (Facebook groups, Substack communities, local forums) where your ideal clients spend time. Show up as an expert answer questions, share value, don't pitch.

Stage 3 Scaling Volume or Value

40+ clients, established reputation, consistent referrals. You want to either fill a waitlist, raise your rates, or launch a group program.
Priority 1

Authority Content

Podcast appearances, guest articles, local press, and partnerships with aligned brands. This is the tier that shifts perception from "good practitioner" to "the practitioner everyone recommends."

Priority 2

Care Team Referral Network

Build formal relationships with OB/GYNs, integrative MDs, therapists, and women's health physios who share your client profile. One strong referral relationship can fill your calendar indefinitely.

Priority 3

Group Program Launch

If you want to scale revenue without adding hours, a well-designed group program is the answer. The marketing for this is a targeted campaign email list, social, and partner promotions.

Stage 4 Building a Brand

You're the known practitioner in your niche. You're expanding to virtual, building a team, or creating a signature program or course.
Priority 1

Multi-Channel Content Engine

You can now invest in full-stack content: YouTube, podcast, long-form articles, cross-platform social. At this stage you need a team or agency to manage the output.

Priority 2

Paid Acquisition

With a proven offer and strong conversion rate, paid channels (Meta ads, Google ads) become viable. Don't run paid ads until you have a high-converting landing page and clear offer.

Priority 3

Partnerships & Licensing

Your methodology can be licensed to other practitioners, or packaged into a corporate wellness program. This is how the highest-impact practitioners reach thousands instead of hundreds.

The rule that cuts through all of this

Before you add any new channel, ask: do I have proof that my current clients are satisfied? If yes, do I have a system to generate referrals from them? If yes, do I have a website that converts the traffic I'm sending to it?

If the answer to any of those is no, don't add a new channel. Fix the foundation. Every channel will underperform if your positioning is unclear, your website doesn't convert, or your client experience doesn't generate testimonials naturally.

The channel is the amplifier. It amplifies what's already there good or bad.

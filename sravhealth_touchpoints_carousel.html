Slide 2 / 9 Touchpoint 1: Search
Srav Health
02 / 09
1
Touchpoint · Search

She Googles
a symptom.

🔍
"Why am I exhausted even after 8 hours of sleep" · "extreme fatigue and low energy during the day" · "Weird periods, bloating, poor sleep natural ways to help"
What she's doing
Searching for an answer, not a practitioner
She doesn't search "Ayurvedic practitioner near me" first. She searches her symptom. Your job is to be the answer to that search.
She finds you through your blog, not your homepage.
What to do
Write symptom-first blog posts targeting her exact search
Title posts the way she thinks (use google keyword planner), not the way you practise. Use Google Search Console to find what she's already typing.
❌ "Ayurveda for Vata imbalance"
✅ "Why you're always tired and cold an intuitive explanation"
The conversion moment
End every post with one clear next step
She's read your article and feels understood for the first time. Don't send her back to Google. Give her a reason to stay a lead magnet, a quiz, or a booking link.
One CTA per post. Always the same one.
Slide 3 / 9 Touchpoint 2: Social Discovery
Srav Health
03 / 09
2
Touchpoint · Social

She scrolls.
You stop her.

What she's actually doing on social
She's not looking for a practitioner. She's consuming content. Your post needs to make her feel in 3 seconds that you understand her problem better than anyone else she's seen.
Passive intent
What stops the scroll
The first line. Name her symptom, her frustration, or a belief she holds. Not your credentials. Not a caption starting with "I'm so excited to share..."
First line is everything
Content that converts at this stage
Before/after frameworks. Myth-busting posts. "Why you're still tired after trying everything." Carousels that teach one thing clearly. Short videos where she sees your face and hears your voice.
Trust-building format
The only CTA that works on social
Don't link out in the post body the algorithm suppresses it. End with a question or "comment [word] and I'll send you the guide." This generates replies, boosts reach, and starts a conversation.
Comment → DM → book
Social doesn't book clients. It builds the trust that makes booking feel safe.

3
Touchpoint · Word of Mouth

Someone
sends her to you.

The moment
A friend, a doctor, or a colleague mentions your name
This is the highest-trust entry point. She arrives already believing you can help. Your job is not to convince it's to confirm.
What she does next
She Googles your name and lands on your website or profile
She's looking for confirmation. Does what she sees match what she was told about you? Your positioning, your client stories, your face.
She was told you help women like me. Is that true? Does this feel right?
How to generate more of this
Make the referral ask a natural part of your care structure
At week 6–8, when outcomes are visible: "If you know someone going through what you were please send them my way." Not an afterthought. A designed moment.
Slide 5 / 9 Touchpoint 4: Website
Srav Health
05 / 09
4
Touchpoint · Your Website

She lands
and evaluates.

Your homepage must do this
Name her symptom in the headline. Not your modality. Her problem.
Show her face and voice. A short video or a personal photo builds trust in seconds.
One clear next step. Book a call, download the guide, or join the list. Not three options.
Specific client outcomes. Not "improved wellbeing" "her energy returned in 6 weeks."
The 8-second rule
She decides in 8 seconds if she's in the right place. Your headline either says "yes, this is for me" or she's gone.
What kills conversions
✕
Headline leads with your credential or modality name
✕
Multiple CTAs competing for attention on one page
✕
No social proof visible above the fold
✕
Booking page requires too many steps to get to
✕
Vague outcome language "balance," "harmony," "wellness"
She's asking three questions
Is this person for someone like me?
Has this worked for people like me?
What do I do next?
Slide 6 / 9 Touchpoint 5: Lead Magnet
Srav Health
06 / 09
5
Touchpoint · Lead Magnet

She's interested.
Not ready yet.

The situation
Most visitors won't book on the first visit
She found you, she likes you, she's not ready. Life is busy. The lead magnet is the bridge it captures her email so you stay in her life until she is ready.
What works
Solve one specific problem in under 10 minutes
A quiz, a short guide, a checklist. Title it in her language. The more specifically it names her situation, the higher the conversion.
"The 5-minute Prakriti quiz for women with chronic fatigue"
What doesn't work
Generic lead magnets nobody downloads
An "Ayurveda 101 guide." A generic "wellness checklist." These don't speak to anyone specifically, so they don't convert.
❌ "Introduction to Ayurveda" ✅ "Why your gut issues keep coming back and what Ayurveda sees that labs miss"
The rule
The lead magnet earns her email. The welcome sequence earns her trust.
The download is not the goal. It's the start of a conversation. What happens in the next 10 days determines whether she books not the download itself.

Touchpoint · Email Nurture

You stay present
until she's ready.

Day 0
Email 1 Immediate
Your guide + what to do with it
Deliver the lead magnet. Add one sentence about what comes next. Sets the expectation that you'll be in touch.
Day 2
Email 2
The thing most practitioners get wrong about your symptom
Pure education. No sell. Builds credibility and reminds her why she signed up.
Email 3 Day 5
A client story (with her permission)
Outcome-led. She sees herself in the story. Social proof without sounding like a testimonial page.
Email 4 Day 8
The one question worth asking yourself
Thought-provoking. Creates a moment of self-reflection that makes her feel you understand her inner world.
Day 10
Email 5 The invitation
If you're ready to take this further here's how
First and only CTA in the sequence. Soft, not pushy. A discovery call framed as a conversation, not a sales pitch.
The rule
Four emails of pure value before a single ask. She should feel educated, not marketed to.
Slide 8 / 9 Touchpoint 7: Discovery Call

08 / 09
7
Touchpoint · Discovery Call

She decides.
You listen.

What this call is not
Not a sales pitch. Not a consultation.
It's a fit conversation for both of you
She's deciding if she trusts you. You're deciding if you can genuinely help her.
The best closing technique is making her feel heard, not persuading her.
The structure that works
5 min: Where is she now? What has she tried?
5 min: What would change if this was resolved?
5 min: Your approach specific to what she's shared
5 min: Next steps and offer, clearly stated
Remove the risk
Make it free and friction-free
20 minutes. Free. No obligation.
Book directly via Calendly no email back-and-forth
Send a reminder 24 hours before with what to expect
If she doesn't show, one follow-up message only
After the call
Send a summary within 2 hours
Recap what she shared in her words
Your specific recommendation for her
One link to book and pay. Nothing else.
The discovery call doesn't close clients. The journey before it does. By the time she's on the call, the decision is mostly made.

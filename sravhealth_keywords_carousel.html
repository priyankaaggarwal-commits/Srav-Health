The core insight

Your client starts
with a feeling,
not a modality.

She does not search "Ayurvedic practitioner." She searches the thing that is keeping her up at night. Your job is to be the answer to that search — which means writing in her language, not yours.

What she never searches
Methodology
"Ayurvedic practitioner near me"
Tradition
"Vata Pitta dosha imbalance help"
Credential
"NAMA certified Ayurveda consultation"
Service
"Panchakarma detox programme online"
What she actually searches
Symptom
"why am I exhausted even after 8 hours sleep"
Frustration
"bloating fatigue anxiety — are these connected"
Situation
"hormonal imbalance after coming off the pill"
Failed by system
"labs normal but still exhausted and wired"

Step 01 · Free · Takes 5 minutes

Google
Autocomplete.

Real searches. Real women. Right now.
→
Open Google in an incognito window
Incognito shows you what everyone searches — not what your personalised history suggests. This matters.
→
Type the beginning of a symptom your clients present with
Do not complete the search. Watch what autocomplete suggests. Each suggestion is a real search term real women are typing right now.
Type: "why am I always tired and" → autocomplete shows: "cold", "bloated", "anxious", "gaining weight"
→
Copy every suggestion that matches what your clients feel
These are your blog post titles, your Instagram hooks, your homepage headline. Not metaphors. The actual words she types.
Do this for every symptom cluster you work with. 20 minutes generates your content brief for the next 3 months.
Slide 4 / 8 — Step 2: Search Console
2
Srav Health
04 / 08
Step 02 · Free · Your own data

Google
Search Console.

See what's already bringing people to you.
→
Go to search.google.com/search-console — it's free
If you do not have it set up, do it today. It shows you exactly which search terms people used to find your site. Historical data only exists from the day you install it — so the longer you wait, the more data you lose.
→
Go to Performance → Search Queries
Sort by impressions. You will see which searches your site appears for — even if nobody clicked yet. Those high-impression, low-click terms are your highest-opportunity content gaps.
High impressions, low clicks = Google thinks you're relevant but your title isn't compelling enough to click.
→
Write one blog post targeting your top impression term
This is your fastest path to more organic traffic — you are already ranking for it, just not converting the impression into a click.
Install Search Console before anything else. You cannot see data from before installation — every week without it is data lost permanently.

3

Step 03 · No tool needed · Most overlooked

Your
intake forms.

The best keyword research you will ever do.
→
Pull your last 10 client intake forms
Find the field where clients describe how they felt before they found you. Read every answer. Do not paraphrase. Do not clean up the language.
→
Copy their exact phrases — unedited
The words your clients use to describe their own experience are almost always more powerful content material than anything a keyword tool generates. This is because they are searching those words too.
"Exhausted by 2pm no matter how early I sleep" · "wired at night but can't wake up" · "bloated every afternoon since my 30s"
→
Turn each phrase into a blog title or Instagram hook
Not "Ayurveda for fatigue." The exact phrase. "Why you're exhausted by 2pm no matter how much you sleep — and what Ayurveda sees that bloodwork misses."
The best positioning is discovered, not invented. Your clients' words are the most accurate map to their world — and to the search bar.
Slide 6 / 8 — Step 4: People Also Ask
4
Srav Health
06 / 08
Step 04 · Free · Instant content brief

"People
Also Ask."

Google's gift to content creators.
→
Search any symptom relevant to your practice
Scroll past the top results. Look for the "People Also Ask" box — a dropdown of questions real users searched alongside your term. This is not Google's guess. It is actual user behaviour data.
→
Each question is a content brief
A blog post that directly answers one People Also Ask question — with the question as the title — will rank faster and more predictably than almost any other content format at the early stage.
Search "hormonal fatigue women" → PAA shows: "Why am I so tired all the time as a woman?" → That is your next blog title.
→
Click each question — more appear below
The PAA box expands as you click. A single 5-minute session generates 20–30 question-led content ideas, all backed by real search demand.
One PAA session per month keeps your content calendar full for 4–6 weeks. Low effort. High relevance.

Put it into practice

Same topic.
Different language.
Different results.

Every example below covers the same Ayurvedic topic. The right-hand version leads with her experience, not your methodology.

❌ Methodology-first — she scrolls past
✕
"Vata imbalance: causes, symptoms and Ayurvedic remedies"
She does not know she has Vata imbalance. She will never search this.
✕
"Ayurveda for hormonal health in women"
Too broad. No emotional hook. Nobody searches this with urgency.
✕
"The benefits of Panchakarma detoxification"
She does not know what Panchakarma is. This does not help her find you.
✕
"Dinacharya: building a daily Ayurvedic routine"
Sanskrit title. She will not search this. It feels like homework, not help.
✅ Symptom-first — she stops and reads
✓
"Why you're always cold, anxious, and exhausted — and what it actually means"
She recognises herself immediately. This is what she types at 11pm.
✓
"Labs came back normal but you still feel terrible. Here's why."
High-intent. This is the sentence in her head. She clicks immediately.
✓
"How a 5-day gut reset changed my client's energy for the first time in 3 years"
Outcome-led. Specific. Credible. Curiosity-driving without being clickbait.
✓
"The morning routine that took my client from 3am wake-ups to sleeping through"
She sees the outcome she wants. The methodology is revealed inside.
The Ayurvedic framework is the answer. Her symptom is always the headline.

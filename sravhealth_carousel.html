Slide 3 of 6 Stage 1: Foundation
Stage 01 · Foundation
03 / 06

Get up.
Get running.
Don't overbuild.

0–15 active clients · $0–$40K gross revenue
Clear, symptom-led positioning
One specific client. One urgent problem she can't solve elsewhere. Written in her language, not yours.
Website built for SEO from day one
Symptom-led headline. Single clear CTA. A blog post targeting her exact search query.
One lead magnet + email list
A short guide or quiz. Free up to 1,000 subscribers on ConvertKit. Capture interest before it disappears.
One channel done consistently
Instagram for most practitioners. LinkedIn if you're serving a professional audience. One. Not three.
Build social proof one client at a time
Ask for a Google review after they mention a positive change. Not on a schedule. At the right moment.
Must non-negotiable at this stage
Should high value, do soon
Slide 4 of 6 Stage 2: Growth
Stage 02 · Growth
04 / 06

Take stock.
Build systems.

15–40 active clients · $40K–$90K gross revenue
Audit first
—
Is this the direction you want? Before building systems, confirm the offer is right.
—
Pull your last 10 intake forms. Use your clients' exact words to sharpen positioning.
—
Build 3 client case studies. Anonymised outcomes are your most powerful marketing asset.
—
Review your pricing. Most Stage 2 practitioners are underpriced. Check the four-quadrant model.
Then expand reach
—
Email campaigns monthly. Educational, not promotional. Builds warm audience for programs.
—
Retargeting ($100–200/month). Show up to website visitors who didn't book. Highest-ROI at this stage.
—
Post-session follow-up flows. 48hr check-in. 3-week rebooking prompt. 6-week referral ask.
—
Design your first group program. Don't launch until you have 200+ subscribers and 5 strong outcomes.
"What got you to Stage 2 was clinical excellence. What takes you through it is systems."
